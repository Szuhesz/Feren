*Let sleeping arachas lie.*

-Cautionary phrase
***

Arachas, or crab spiders, are large insectoids with a distinctly large abdomen. Described as an extremely venomous, its variants are found in both desert and swamp regions. The varieties found in the Thernian Lowlands tend to have mouths that resemble a large orchid.

Powerful pincers, a maw filled with razor-sharp teeth and venom glands packed with deadly toxins, these features constitute the arachas' deadly arsenal. People and farm animals make up an important part of these creatures' diet.

Once native to the far south, this invasive species migrated north over the course of centuries, adjusting as it went to new climates and temperatures. It found damp woodlands and swamps most hospitable and made them its home, making use of the much and moss found there as blankets during its winter hibernation. The arachas hides its unprotected, sack-like abdomen under a covering of hollow tree-trunks worn on its back.
