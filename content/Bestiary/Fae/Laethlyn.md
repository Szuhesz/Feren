*Only the desperate would call on the 'mercy' of the Laethlyn. She has none. And if she decides that you've committed a crime against the land she protects, you may suffer a worse fate than any you were trying to escape.*

-Professor Taliah Nersk
***

Human like in many ways, yet they bolster features of a deer. Parts of their body covered in fur, eyes blank white, without an iris and an antler on their head. They seem to command nature, as plants in their vicinity come to life, aiding their movement.

The Laethlyn, a powerful fae creature, is assumed to be even more deeply connected to nature than its Fae counterparts. They brook no intrusion by those who do not have those interests at heart. The Laethlyn tends to plants and beasts of the wilderness, raising them to be both companions and defenses against incursions by the so-called civilized world.

Folktales say they also attempt to heal those creatures who've succumbed to darkness. However, if healing is impossible, they consider a quick death a mercy and an obligation. Their sentiment regarding curses like vampirism or therianthropism are similar, something to be cleansed off the face of the lands.

A rare specie with barely any reports of its sightings, yet retellings of those who came out alive of such an encounter often recite familiar features and behavior. Scholars speculate there might be only one of these per deep wilderness.
