
There was nothing, but darkness, a gap without design  
With one hand they create, while the other untwine  
First came the children, twelve to fill the void  
Each after their own, desires loudly voiced  

It was cold and empty, timeless yet eternal  
In their hands lay a spark, wrapped in golden kernel  
Their children watched in awe as the promised seed  
Burst forth with a world they shall succeed  

Endless fields of land and wave, beautiful and untamed  
To make it whole came us, this world forever claimed  
Creations of their will, made in their perfect image  
The children to guide us, on our endless voyage  

Embody them all, for their design is plain  
The divine within us shall ever remain  
Whether it’s you, or those yet to rise  
Their light endures beyond our demise  

Filled with drive and purpose, each seeking to reign  
To shape the world alone, their marks ingrained  
Conflict took root, and their vision waned  
As their quarrels deepened, balance was strained  

Bickering and strife, their bonds fell apart  
Scales of balance tipped with breaking heart  
They fought and bled, leaving scars so deep  
A world undone, by gods who could not keep  

Amid the ruin, the Maker stood still  
Betrayed by half, yet bound by will  
The loyal wept as the traitors withdrew  
Their hands stained red, the balance askew  

Their essence unravelled to heal what was torn  
A world remade, though forever worn  
The faithful mourned, the betrayers in pain  
Bound to the scars of their treacherous gain
