The Rhovand tribes trace their origins to an age long forgotten. Their Ancestors once walked in harmony with the land's Spirits, guiding the Rhovand toward prosperity. Ancient sunken ruins and sacred groves whisper of their former glory, while the Songs they once sang carry tales of past triumphs and those yet to unfold.

Each tribe was formed in devotion to a Great Aspect, powerful Spirits that govern the world. The Ancestors built sacred shrines in their honor and wandered The Spirit's Walk in their names. Their history is all but lost, preserved only in the Songs passed down through generations and the Songstones, their runes and murals whispering the tales of the past.

Today's Clans are but a shadow of their former glory. Hundreds of Lunar Arcs ago, The Eclipse marked the fall of the Ancestors. The Elders of the Rhovand disagree on the interpretation of the Songs that recount those events, yet one truth remains: the Dark Spirits, the Revenants, played a role. Some claim that a small faction of the Ancestors willingly sided with them, while others believe the Ancestors were deceived and led astray, unknowingly severing their bond with the Spirits.

Without the guidance and wisdom of the Spirits, survival has become a relentless struggle for the tribes. Internal conflict only intensifies the already dire conditions, as tribes turn against one another. To make matters worse, a disease known as the Hollowing is spreading across The Spirit's Walk, leaving Hollow Land behind. Far to the east are the first signs of this curse, where the land meets the waves, the Spirits are abandoning the Rhovand and their homeland, condemning them to certain ruin.
## Societal Structure

At the head of the Flock are the Elders, seasoned individuals who bear the weight of many lunar arcs' worth of wisdom and knowledge. They serve as spiritual leaders, storytellers, lore keepers, and decision-makers. Here, the Ancestors' Favour comes into play, for to become an Elder, one must bear it. From a young age, certain individuals exhibit abilities shared by only a select few, allowing them to wield and manipulate the forces of the land, granting them great power. It is believed that the Ancestor Spirits watch over these individuals closely, recognizing the immense potential they carry. Other significant roles include the shamans and druids, who are responsible for maintaining communication with the Spirits and warding off the Revenants. In addition, stable masters hold great respect, as they oversee the beasts of burden upon which the clans heavily rely. Warriors and hunters are also highly esteemed for their obvious contributions, and in such communities, where each individual is vital to the survival of the whole, no role goes unappreciated.
# Rhovand Naming Conventions

Unlike the Forsaken, the Rhovand tribes do not use family names. The reason is simple: their population is far smaller. A single Flock's numbers are no more than a few thousand. Naturally, names are often repeated, but to avoid confusion, individuals are identified by their parentage, for example, "son of _name_" or "daughter of _name_". These conventions are not used by everyone. As mentioned earlier, though rare, some Forsaken do assimilate. These outsiders to the Spirit's Walk often bring their own customs with them, and among these, the use of family names occasionally finds its way into Rhovand society. As for titles and monikers, they are very much a part of Rhovand culture. These often take the place of parentage in one's name. They can be simple and light-hearted or carry great weight and command respect. One thing remains constant, titles are always earned and bestowed. They are never used in mockery.
