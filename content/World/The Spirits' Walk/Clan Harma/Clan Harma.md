Clan Harma is devoted to the Great Aspect of the Wolf, embodying strength, loyalty, and cunning, traits essential for survival in the harsh north-western reaches of The Spirit's Walk. The ever-present threat of The Forsaken, with their unnatural machinations and eerie structures, looms over the land. Meanwhile, Clan Morco, forced to abandon their Ancestral Lands and push westward, now quarrels over what little remains of yours. With the wilderness growing ever more perilous, your people have been shaped by hardship, adapting into a clan of unmatched versatility. Just as wolves employ strategy in their hunts, the clan would favor cunning and wit, particularly in diplomacy and warfare. Ambushes and guerrilla tactics would be central to their combat style. However, when the moment to strike arrives, a wolf's strength must be unleashed without restraint. Clan Harma is perhaps the most united of the Ancestor Clans still in existence. The Spirit of the Wolf demands unity, for a lone wolf is one cast out, abandoned to die. Thus, the pack lies at the heart of the clan's culture. Each Flock has its own specialized role, each dependent on the others to endure the trials of daily life. Natural leaders emerge through hardship and the trials of survival, serving as examples for the rest. This phenomenon is evident among the Flocks and even in smaller communities, where leadership shifts over time as strength, wisdom, and circumstance dictate.
## Tradition and Culture

Each clan upholds its own traditions, but perhaps the most renowned among Clan Harma is the Trial of the Wolf. To earn their place as a full member, each youth must undergo a rite of passage. Whether a solitary journey into the wild, a test of endurance, or a sacred hunt, proving their worth and their bond with the Spirit of the Wolf. Those who pass the trial are awarded a metal bracelet, its ends not fully joined, with two wolf heads facing each other. Some clan members might be spiritually or physically marked by the wolf, either through tattoos, totems, or even unique physical traits. These individuals are called Wolfkin and considered to carry the Wolf Spirit's mark, and blessed among their clan members. Another notable tradition is the Hunt of the Full Moons, a sacred rite marking the beginning of the hunting season. During this time, families and friends come together to hunt their prize in the wilds. Following certain victories, the clan may hold the Wolf's Feast, where the finest portions of the hunt are first offered to the Elders, then to the warriors and hunters. It is a time of storytelling, celebration, and the passing down of wisdom. Those of higher rank within the clan often wear wolf pelts or fur as clothing. It is considered a sacred honor to be permitted to don the animal's fur, signifying a direct connection to the Wolf Spirit.
