The Luirens Dynasty is a fragile empire, a patchwork of restless provinces constantly on the brink of rebellion. At its helm stands Artholas Luirens, the "Iron Hand," a ruler whose might and righteousness serve as both shield and sword against the chaos threatening his dominion. The nation is ruled by an oligarchy, a council of high nobles, each commanding their own territory with near-autonomous authority. However, the people of Luirens were not always united. Centuries ago, the Therlands consisted of multiple nations, each racing for resources and dominance. Callace Luirens, the head of his dynasty at the time, brought his final enemy, Styria, to its knees. With that victory, he declared himself Emperor, the rightful ruler of his dynasty and all of the Therlands.
## Silverkeep

The capital of Styria was founded long before the revelations even took place. Over generations, it grew and expanded, accommodating an ever increasing population. Named after the region's vast reserves of ores and minerals, Silverkeep has become a city of immense wealth, its riches rivalling even those of the crown itself. Silverkeep is not only a city of wealth but also a seat of power. House Alnwick has ruled over the region for so long that few even remember a time before their reign. Styria is composed of noble houses, each governing their own domain. Unlike the monarchy of the Luirens, Styria operates as an oligarchy, where the heads of these houses convene in Silverkeep, making decisions as a unified council.    
## Rimecrest

The northernmost settlement is Rimecrest, a frozen stronghold marking the edge of the Whispering Hills, locked in eternal ice. Beyond Rimecrest, only scattered mining settlements remain, where the riches of the Gray Peaks are unearthed and flow into the province. These settlements rely on Rimecrest's industry, as much of the extracted ore is forged into tools essential for their operations. Skilled craftsmen call this frozen town home, their expertise fuelling the province's relentless pursuit of wealth and survival. For a decade now, House Rowe has held dominion over Rimecrest, rising from the embers of House Wells's disgrace. Their grip is firm but fair, their rule bolstered by the goodwill of the townsfolk. None more so than Lady Amelia, whose keen eye and measured hand have earned her the people's favour. She walks among them, not as some distant noble, but as one who understands their toils. Blacksmiths, masons, and weavers alike find fortune under her patronage, their crafts flourishing in her stead. In Rimecrest, her name carries weight, not by fear, but by respect.
