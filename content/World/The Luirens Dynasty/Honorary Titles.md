Honorary titles are granted to both nobles and common citizens. They often reflect one's occupation and speak to their merit or worth. Holders frequently fulfill crucial roles within noble houses or organisations such as guilds. These titles appear in contexts like noble councils, guild leadership, and similar institutions. For lower class citizens, honorary titles provide one of the few avenues of social mobility, offering a path toward recognition and, in some cases, entry into the nobility. Below you can find some of them.
# Nobility

### Master of Coin

Serves as the region's treasurer and bookkeeper.
### Master of the Hunt

Serves as an overseer to the region's noble hunts.
### Master of Whispers

Serves as the region's intelligence advisor and spymaster.
### Master of Ships

Serves as the region's naval officer, supervising and in command of its fleet.
### Lord Commander

Serves as the region's military commander, particularly in land based warfare.
### Master of Law

Serves as the region's legal advisor, who also manages its dungeons and supervises law enforcement.
### Lord Chamberlain

Serves as a custodian to the noble chambers and their home. Handles logistics.
### Herald

Serves as an announcer within the region's nobility, usually at ceremonies.
### Preceptor

Serves as a tutor for the nobility.
### Grand Falconer

Serves as the noble falconer, who personally oversees the noble Aerie.
### Master of Horse

Overseer of noble horses and carriages.
### Master of Road

Maintains infrastructure, tolls and travel permissions. Controls movement across the realm.
### Quill Master

Scribe and record keeper of a Noble House. Oversees matters and records of tax, inheritance, bloodline registries, etc... Keeps their House's history.
### Seal Bearer

Custodian of noble seals. Validates and handles authority and merit.
### Census Warden

Oversees population counts, birth/death rates, handles tithing.
### Imperial Reeve

Manages the Emperor's lands and collects taxes for him. Enforces quotas.
### Ashkeeper

Maintains heresy records, trial transcripts, confessions.
### Warden of Chains

Overseer of prisons, forced labor and political prisoners. Directly works under the Master of Law.

# Guild

### Guild Clerk

Manages guild apprenticeships, enforces guild law.
### Tallyman

Responsible for tracking guild production quotas, labor hours, output.
### Quorumwright

Organizes and officiates guild councils, votes, political strategy.
### Guild Herald

Public voice and representative of a guild.
### Trade Envoy

Represents the guild in foreign courts and with nobles.
### Oathwright

Administers binding guild oaths, contracts.
### Sanctioner

Handles guild punishment, from exile to execution.
### Crown Factor

A guild appointed official within a noble court to manage trade interests.
### Privileged Artisan

A master craftsman elevated to near noble status.
### High Falconer

Member of the High Aerie, manages courier birds.
***
# Other
### Knight

A sworn warrior bound by oath to the granter of this title.
### Chain Bearer

a former prisoner sworn into service.
### Fire Witness

One who survived an execution and later legitimized.
