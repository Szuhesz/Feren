This is where our story begins. Styria lies in the north-eastern corner of the Therlands, just west of the Gray Peaks. Its harsh climate leaves half the province buried in snow all cycle, making survival a constant struggle for its people. Ultimately, this unforgiving land led to their downfall in the Unification Wars, the relentless and superior Styrian warriors could no longer sustain themselves, forcing them to bend the knee. Now, the cycle is 164 AR., a century since the Unification Wars, yet old grudges still linger. The people of Styria have not forgotten. However, things have changed. Due to Styria's renowned military prowess, they were granted more favourable terms, at least for a vassal. As the Dynasty's shield against external threats, Styria serves as a border province, tasked with its defence. In return, they are allowed to retain internal autonomy. They were granted lands to the south, offering them the opportunity to sustain themselves to some extent. However, with growing trade opportunities, particularly the import of crops from the capital, Styria has experienced rapid population growth.
