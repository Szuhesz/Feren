The geography of the Therlands is both unique and diverse. Nearly every region differs in terrain, and as a result each functions in its own distinct way.
***
# Gray Peaks

The Gray Peaks are a vast mountain chain stretching for miles, dividing the continent in two. Their depths contain an immense wealth of mineral veins, vital to the functioning of the Dynasty. Deeper still lie the tunnels of the Cordor, small and stout humans who have, over the ages, adapted to the underground life they carved for themselves. Finally, the range serves as a natural shield, forming an almost impassable barrier between the Dynasty and its most dangerous threats, such as the Rhovand and the Valar tribes.
***
# Styria

Styria is a vast land divided into two major regions. It remains snow covered for most of the cycle and supports a unique ecosystem shaped by the cold.
***
# Whispering Hills

To the north lie the Whispering Hills, named for the constant winds that sweep across the hill strewn lands and produce their howling sound. The climate here is so cold that most flora cannot survive, leaving room for unusually large beasts and rare, distinctive flowers.
***
# Ancestral Woods

To the south lie the Ancestral Woods, home to trees older than most and wildlife unlike any other within the Dynasty. To this day, its forests remain a vital resource for the people who dwell there.
***
# Lowlands

Legends and tales claim that the Thernian Lowlands were not always sunken. Some widely told stories say that one of the Divines stumbled and fell while shaping the Therlands. Others claim that Callace Luirens, founder of the Dynasty during the Unification Wars, cleaved the Therlands in two to shield his people from the Styrians.
