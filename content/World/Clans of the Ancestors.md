The Rhovand tribes trace their origins to an age long forgotten. Their Ancestors once walked in harmony with the land's Spirits, guiding the Rhovand toward prosperity. Ancient sunken ruins and sacred groves whisper of their former glory, while the Songs they once sang carry tales of past triumphs and those yet to unfold. Each tribe was formed in devotion to a Great Aspect, powerful Spirits that govern the world. The Ancestors built sacred shrines in their honour and wandered The Spirit's Walk in their names. Their history is all but lost, preserved only in the Songs passed down through generations and the Songstones, their runes and murals whispering the tales of the past. Today's Clans are but a shadow of their former glory. Hundreds of Lunar Arcs ago, The Eclipse marked the fall of the Ancestors. The Elders of the Rhovand disagree on the interpretation of the Songs that recount those events, yet one truth remains: the Dark Spirits, the Revenants, played a role. Some claim that a small faction of the Ancestors willingly sided with them, while others believe the Ancestors were deceived and led astray, unknowingly severing their bond with the Spirits. Without the guidance and wisdom of the Spirits, survival has become a relentless struggle for the tribes. Internal conflict only intensifies the already dire conditions, as tribes turn against one another. To make matters worse, a disease known as the Hollowing is spreading across The Spirit's Walk, leaving Hollow Land behind. Far to the east are the first signs of this curse, where the land meets the waves, the Spirits are abandoning the Rhovand and their homeland, condemning them to certain ruin.
# Clan Harma

Clan Harma is devoted to the Great Aspect of the Wolf, embodying strength, loyalty, and cunning, traits essential for survival in the harsh north-western reaches of The Spirit's Walk. The ever-present threat of The Forsaken, with their unnatural machinations and eerie structures, looms over the land. Meanwhile, Clan Morco, forced to abandon their Ancestral Lands and push westward, now quarrels over what little remains of yours. With the wilderness growing ever more perilous, your people have been shaped by hardship, adapting into a clan of unmatched versatility. Just as wolves employ strategy in their hunts, the clan would favour cunning and wit, particularly in diplomacy and warfare. Ambushes and guerrilla tactics would be central to their combat style. However, when the moment to strike arrives, a wolf's strength must be unleashed without restraint. Clan Harma is perhaps the most united of the Ancestor Clans still in existence. The Spirit of the Wolf demands unity, for a lone wolf is one cast out, abandoned to die. Thus, the pack lies at the heart of the clan's culture. Each Flock has its own specialised role, each dependent on the others to endure the trials of daily life. Natural leaders emerge through hardship and the trials of survival, serving as examples for the rest. This phenomenon is evident among the Flocks and even in smaller communities, where leadership shifts over time as strength, wisdom, and circumstance dictate.
## Tradition and Culture

Each clan upholds its own traditions, but perhaps the most renowned among Clan Harma is the Trial of the Wolf. To earn their place as a full member, each youth must undergo a rite of passage. Whether a solitary journey into the wild, a test of endurance, or a sacred hunt, proving their worth and their bond with the Spirit of the Wolf. Those who pass the trial are awarded a metal bracelet, its ends not fully joined, with two wolf heads facing each other. Some clan members might be spiritually or physically marked by the wolf, either through tattoos, totems, or even unique physical traits. These individuals are called Wolfkin and considered to carry the Wolf Spirit's mark, and blessed among their clan members. Another notable tradition is the Hunt of the Full Moons, a sacred rite marking the beginning of the hunting season. During this time, families and friends come together to hunt their prize in the wilds. Following certain victories, the clan may hold the Wolf's Feast, where the finest portions of the hunt are first offered to the Elders, then to the warriors and hunters. It is a time of storytelling, celebration, and the passing down of wisdom. Those of higher rank within the clan often wear wolf pelts or fur as clothing. It is considered a sacred honour to be permitted to don the animal's fur, signifying a direct connection to the Wolf Spirit.
# Flock Carca

When we speak of Flocks, we refer to the individual communities within a clan. Each Flock consists of a few thousand people who journey together along The Spirit's Walk. These Flocks are bound by the traditions and culture established by the Ancestor Clans. However, despite this shared heritage, it is not uncommon for conflicts to arise between Flocks within a clan. Conflicts that can sometimes escalate into physical confrontations. As previously mentioned, the unity within Clan Harma enables its Flocks to live in relative peace with one another. These strong internal relations foster smooth trading and diplomacy, allowing each Flock to have its voice heard. Flock Carca has always held a role of confrontation within the Clan. Known as the fang, their Flock is renowned for producing legendary warriors whose feats rival those of the Songstones themselves. In recent lunar arcs, however, there has been an increase in violent skirmishes with Clan Morco. To the east of the Howling Wilds, the clan's hunting grounds are contested, and when two hunting parties cross paths, clashes often erupt to determine who claims the prey.
## Societal Structure

At the head of the Flock are the Elders, seasoned individuals who bear the weight of many lunar arcs' worth of wisdom and knowledge. They serve as spiritual leaders, storytellers, lore keepers, and decision-makers. Here, the Ancestors' Favour comes into play, for to become an Elder, one must bear it. From a young age, certain individuals exhibit abilities shared by only a select few, allowing them to wield and manipulate the forces of the land, granting them great power. It is believed that the Ancestor Spirits watch over these individuals closely, recognising the immense potential they carry. Other significant roles include the shamans and druids, who are responsible for maintaining communication with the Spirits and warding off the Revenants. In addition, stable masters hold great respect, as they oversee the beasts of burden upon which the clans heavily rely. Warriors and hunters are also highly esteemed for their obvious contributions, and in such communities, where each individual is vital to the survival of the whole, no role goes unappreciated.
# Further Developments

As your Flock journeys toward The Veins of the Ancestors, word reaches you from your clan, rumours swirl of an outsider who claims to be the Reithalan. The name stirs something deep within you all, echoing the tales told in childhood, stories of one who walks both the lands of the Rhovand and the realm of the Spirits. Whispers spread that the stranger fits the prophecy's description: they know the Spirits of the land and bear the scars of flame upon their body. Could it be? The Song of the Fireborn may yet come to pass. Is this the answer the Ancestor Clans have long sought?
# Rhovand Naming Conventions

Unlike the Forsaken, the Rhovand tribes do not use family names. The reason is simple: their population is far smaller. A single Flock numbers no more than a few thousand. Naturally, names are often repeated, but to avoid confusion, individuals are identified by their parentage, for example, "son of _name_" or "daughter of _name_". These conventions are not used by everyone. As mentioned earlier, though rare, some Forsaken do assimilate. These outsiders to the Spirit's Walk often bring their own customs with them, and among these, the use of family names occasionally finds its way into Rhovand society. As for titles and monikers, they are very much a part of Rhovand culture. These often take the place of parentage in one's name. They can be simple and light-hearted or carry great weight and command respect. One thing remains constant, titles are always earned and bestowed. They are never used in mockery.
