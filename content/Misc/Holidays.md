# Sprouting

Sprouting is celebrated on the 1st of Callas. It heralds the end of winter. The holiday is a festival of the hearth and home, and a celebration of the lengthening days and the early signs of spring.

Celebrations often involve hearthfires, special foods, lighting candles or a bonfire if the weather permitted. Fire and purification is an important part of the festival. The lighting of candles and fires represents the return of warmth and the increasing power of the sun over the coming months. In some regions blessed candles are lit during storms and placed in windows to ward off thunders.

# Flowerbloom

On the 30th of Callas, when the flowers are in bloom for the first time after the long and dreary winter the whole Empire comes together in celebration. Children and young girls make and wear flower crowns, men and boys give flowers to the important women in their lives, be they mothers, aunts, wives daughters or crushes. Small festivals themed around flowers and the true coming of spring are set up in most town squares bringing joy and fun all around. Flowers are often bound with gold spun thread to bring luck and the blessings of the Divines. Cheap yellow died wool in most homes or even straw for those really struggling to get by, still, more affluent nobles do use real gold thread.
***
# Fathers & Mothers Day

Respectively on the 10th of Callas and 10th of Seedcast, humble celebrations are held, each unique to one's family. Children present gifts often handcrafted to their parents, or simple yet heartwarming stage plays are held by them directed by their teachers.

After the Unification War, in comparison to Thaeran history, peaceful times descended these lands. The sudden population boom and disproportionate amount of young people spurred this holiday, one that was adapted like wildfire across all the Empire's regions.
***
# Blossoming

Blossoming, takes place on the night of 14th of Seedcast. It is a fertility festival beginning at dusk  and continuing until dawn. It is also a traditional time to begin, or end, relationships of a physical nature. Food, drink and love are the order of the evening making this celebration a time of many marriages. Bonfires abound, and revelers are encouraged to jump the flames for luck and their own fertility.
***
# Unity Day

On the 19th of Greenwake, in the early days of Suncrest, there is a national holiday that celebrates the achievements and feats of Callace Luriens. Through him, the regions west of the Gray Peaks were united, bringing an age of peace and prosperity.

The day's main spectacle happens in the capital, Crownseat. As a large military parade is held that marches through its largest street, Callace Lane, under the Victory Arch, all the way till the noble palace. In front of which the military might of the Royal Guard is showcased. Legends claim Callace and his army took the same path ages past, when they first returned home. Bloodied, yet victorious.
***
# The Lightbearer's Rite

In the hottest days of Suncrest, on the 15th of Highlight's eve, crowds form and march down streets and paths, carrying fire in all forms possible. These celebrations often involve whole settlements, as these fires are brought to the Church itself, where they are joined in a single, large pyre. It symbolizes the strength of communities, and meant to stand as a reminder of humanity's responsibility. That even in good times, one must preserve the Light.
***
# Feste of the Scythe

This holiday is the harvest-holiday also called the "Feste of the Scythe" or the "Festival of the Harvest", which is celebrated in the first days of Elia, from the 1st throughout the 4th. It is accompanied by parties full of dance and music.
## The Great Ball

A great masquerade ball is held at Crownseat every cycle on the final night of the Harvest Festival. Attendees typically spend months working on their costumes for the occasion.
***
# The Unmasking

The Unmasking is a celebration held every cycle in the great city of Stormspire to commemorate the founding of the city. It consists of ten days of feasting and revelry, and where participants wear elaborate masks. At midnight on the tenth day, the Stormhorn is made to roar and all the celebrates remove their masks together. It starts each cycle on the 26th of Alos lasting until the 4th of Ashfall.
***
# Frostwake

Marks the coldest turning point, on the 16th of Hayrest. People often gather in various activities as they try to "wake" the land. As always, the Church left their stamp on this tradition, as most people join under one activity, which is usually keeping a large pyre of fire going throughout the whole day. Its warmth is said to break the deepest frosts of the land beneath, turning the weather itself.

Scattered and few, some places where the Church's reach is still weak, sacrifices of old are still made. Though not much is known, in the furthest reaches of Styria and Tempest's Reach, one may find heretical practices on this day.
***
# The Hearth Oath

A holiday only celebrated within Styria. In the depths of Deepcold, on the 24th of Hayrest, where life centers around fires, oaths and pacts of old are renewed. Further strengthening the deep traditions and customs revolving around one's word and promise, that is binding over generations to come in this land and its people.
***
# Emberwake

On the 21st of Dithis, the Church celebrates the day humanity received a Divine gift of foresight. Each cycle, people gather to pray and maintain candles on this day, in an effort to pay homage to the Founding Saints, who heard the call of the Divines during the Night of Forewarning. This event set humanity on the right path, led by the Church.
***
# The Great Games

The Great Games are a series of dramatic combat contests held in Tempest's Reach. These are cruel and sadistic shows in which slaves may fight for their freedom. There is no set date for this event, but a declaration in the early days of a cycle by the rulers of these lands, as it often depends on the supply of slaves.
